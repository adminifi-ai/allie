# Naming Notes

The repo is named `allie`.

The strongest naming stack from the brainstorming pass:

- Product: **Allay** or **Allie**
- Repo/current working name: **allie**
- Weird internal harness family: **Wardensight**
- Evidence/reporting subsystem: **Attestor**
- PR/release gate component: **Janus**
- Human assistant persona: **Allie**

## Why `allie`

`allie` is short, warm, memorable, and adjacent to `a11y` without making the whole brand feel like acronym software. It can be a CLI, assistant persona, repo, and product name.

Risk: it is common and person-name shaped. Before public launch, run trademark, package, domain, and adjacent product checks.

## Names To Avoid

- Axe or axe-derived names: collision/confusion with Deque axe.
- Sentry: too much collision with observability/security.
- Gatekeeper: exclusionary connotation.
- Enforcer: compliance-police framing.
- WCAGBot, ADA Auditor, Section508Bot: too narrow and scanner-like.
- A11yCheck, AccessScan: generic and undersells the evidence system.
- Argus: strong in isolation, but likely collides with internal review-stack language and many external tools.
